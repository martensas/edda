\section{Introduction}

The \textbf{Greenlandish Speeches of Attle} (\textlink{Atlamal}) are only preserved in \Regius.  The poem is composed in \Malahattr\ throughout.  Unlike the preceding \textlink{Atlakvida}, it seems actually to have been composed in one of the Norse settlements on Greenland, for in st. 18 the poet makes reference to a “white bear”.  The polar bear (\emph{Ursus maritimus}) is indeed found on Greenland, but not on Iceland or the Scandinavian peninsula.  To what inhospitable northern wastes the Norse had brought the legends about Attle (\emph{Attila})!

The language of the poem is noticably younger than its predecessor; the sound change \emph{vr-} > \emph{r-} is consistently applied.
