%Runic poetry from Sweden and Gotland.

\section{Introduction}

The inscriptions are sorted according to their runological signa as found in the Scandinavian Runic-text Data Base (SRDB).  For most inscriptions the signum derives from the standard corpus edition \emph{Sveriges runinskrifter} (‘Runic inscriptions of Sweden’) and consists of two elements: the abbreviated traditional province and the number given in the corpus edition.  E.g., Sm 39 is the inscription given number 39 in the Småland volume of \emph{Sveriges runinskrifter}.

Inscriptions discovered after the publication of the corpus edition describing the relevant province have instead of the number an abbreviated bibliographical reference to the \emph{editio princeps}.  E.g., Ög Fv1950;341 is a inscription from Östergötland first published in the 1950 volume of \emph{Fornvännen}, page 341.

The province of Scania, although part of Sweden since 1658, was in the Wiking Age and the mediæval period an integral part of Denmark.  As such its metrical inscriptions are found under the Danish section.

\subsection{Abbreviations of Swedish provinces}

\begin{itemize}
  \item G = Gotland
  \item Nä = Närke
  \item Sm = Småland
  \item Sö = Södermanland
  \item U = Uppland
  \item Vg = Västergötland
  \item Vs = Västmanland
  \item Ög = Östergötland
  \item Öl = Öland
\end{itemize}

\section{Ög 165 (Skänninge)}

\begin{flushright}%
\textbf{Dating:} C11th

\textbf{Meter:} \Fornyrdislag
\end{flushright}%

\subsection{Introduction}

A tall standing stone from Skänninge.  The carver Thurkettle is the same as that of Ög 81.

\subsection{Transliteration}

§ A \textbf{(·) þurun · risti · auk × þiR × bruþr × suniʀ :} \\
§ B \textbf{× tusta × iftiʀ · sin · faþur} \\
§ C \textbf{× þurkil×k rist × stin : þansi : aufti : tusta :}

\subsection{Text}

\bvg\bva[]%
\alst{Þ}ȯr·unn \edtrans{rêsti}{raised [the stone]}{\Bfootnote{The wording is so formulaic that the object can be left out without any injury to the sense.}} \hld\ auk \alst{þ}êʀ brǿðr, &
\alst{s}yniʀ Tosta, \hld\ ęptiʀ \alst{s}inn fǫður. &
Þȯr·kell ’k rêst stên þannsi øpti Tosta.\eva

\bvb Thurun raised [the stone] along with her brothers \\
the sons of Toste, after their father. \\
I, Thurkettle, carved this stone after Toste.\evb\evg
